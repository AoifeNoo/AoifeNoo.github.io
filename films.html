Herstory: Mary Elmes

We were commissioned by RTE to make a short film about Mary Elmes as part of the Herstory movement which tells the stories of historic, mythical and modern women.

Mary Elmes, a Corkwoman and Trinity scholar, turned her back on a brilliant academic career to volunteer in two of the 20th century’s worst conflicts. During the Spanish Civil War, she set up and ran children’s hospitals. During World War II she continued to help refugees and later risked her life to save Jewish children from deportation.

The short stop motion animated film was screened in the Epic Museum and broadcast on RTE2. It is available to watch on the RTE Player. Click below to watch the film in full.
